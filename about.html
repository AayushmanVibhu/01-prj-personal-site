About Me

I'm a Computer Science student who enjoys building ideas that scale, not just technically, but in meaning and impact. I'm especially interested in how artificial intelligence can be used thoughtfully, not merely to automate tasks, but to enrich human life and deepen everyday experiences. I don't see technology as something that should simply make life easier—I'm drawn to building systems that make life richer, more intentional, and more human.

What I'm Focused On Now

  • Leveraging AI to build tools that enhance human experience, not just efficiency
  • Strengthening my computer science fundamentals to design systems that scale responsibly
  • Building projects that explore the intersection of technology, behavior, and real-world impact

How I Think About Technology

  • Technology should support people, not replace them
  • AI is most powerful when it augments human judgment and creativity
  • Systems should scale without losing their human core
  • Long-term impact matters more than short-term convenience

Beyond Code

I think deeply about growth, patience, and long-term thinking. I'm interested in how small decisions compound into meaningful outcomes over time. I value curiosity, discipline, and intentionality, and I try to bring those into everything I build. Most of my learning happens through building. I like taking abstract ideas, breaking them down into systems, and experimenting until they work in the real world. I'm comfortable sitting in uncertainty, iterating slowly, and letting projects evolve over time.
